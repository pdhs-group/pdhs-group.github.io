Battery Technology

Particle technology is essential for electric mobility. The microstructure formed by the interactions between active materials and conductive additives is central to battery performance and capacity. Understanding and optimizing these interactions at particle level is crucial to improving energy storage, promoting efficient charge/discharge cycles and ultimately advancing the development of electric mobility.

Microplastics

Microplastics, tiny plastic particles less than five millimetres in size, pose a significant environmental threat as they can enter water sources, harm aquatic ecosystems and potentially enter the food chain. During Magnetic Seeded Filtration, magnetic particles are strategically introduced into the water and their affinity for microplastic particles facilitates selective aggregation. By applying an external magnetic field, these agglomerated particles can be efficiently separated from the water. This innovative approach is an example of how particle technology can be used to address today's environmental challenges with precision and effectiveness.

About

Particles shape our world and everyday life: Macroscopic material and product qualities are determined by microscopic interactions.

Our goal is to answer pressing questions about agglomeration and breakage phenomena, when multiple materials come together. To achieve this, we believe in the synergy between theoretical modeling and hands-on experimentation.

Understanding the dynamics of these heterogeneous interfaces is crucial for optimizing manufacturing processes, improving material properties, and driving innovation in areas such as energy storage.
